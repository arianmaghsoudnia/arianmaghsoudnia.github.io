Optimizing Single Crystal PV Panels Using Low Emission Films

Bachelor Thesis

Today’s world is challenging with an ever-increasing demand for energy. Because of restrictions and shortage in fossil fuel resources and hazards that nuclear energy can cause, one should look for a way to obviate this need to fulfill the domestic and industrial need for energy through clean and sustainable sources of energy. In this process, one should consider all environmental aspects. Regarding these, extensive research has been done in the field of renewable energies like Wind, Solar, and Geothermal energies. However, each of the above-mentioned sources is facing some impediments in the way of becoming available in an industrial grid-scale amount; limited capacity factor and intermittent accessibility are among these problems.

Among various types of clean energies, this work focuses on increasing solar panel’s capacity factor, to be further used industrially.

Solar cells convert solar irradiation directly to electricity. The fundamentals of a working solar cell are based on a phenomenon called the photovoltaic effect, which is based on the free electron or hole movement through different states of energy in a P_N junction. Solar irradiation can cause free electrons to cross the forbidden bandgap to higher energy levels. The main point in this process is that only specific wavelengths can initiate this effect. The energy of a photon is proportional to its wavelength, meaning that with merely increasing the irradiation, higher efficiency would not necessarily be obtained; instead specific near-green lights are perfect for mono-crystal photovoltaic cells which were used in this work.

To improve photovoltaic panel grid-scale utilization there are some obstacles. Some of which are space limitations for the collector surface and thus less incident irradiation, limited solar irradiation hours within a day, soiling and dust accumulation, storing electrical electricity, and decreased solar panel efficiency due to increased surface temperature.

One of the most important problems of PV panels is the dropped capacity factor due to the surface temperature increase. This phenomenon entails the utilization of cooling methods in order to restore efficiency; therefore, a significant amount of energy would be used to reduce the temperature.

Knowing that the greatest portion of radiation heat is transferred to the panel via Infrared lights, which does not produce electricity in the panel- cutting off this band of wavelengths can retain the panel from excessive heat. Therefore, placing a light filter in the way of incident irradiation can be helpful. In this project, various commercial low-emission films have been used to reduce the amount of infrared light, resulting in increased capacity factor in equal irradiations. Lessening the concern over surface temperature, solar concentrators were used to further increase the amount of incident irradiation. If necessary, solar trackers can be applied to the concentrator system.

The other idea in this project is to use the reflected useful solar irradiation to increase the panel's capacity factor. In this scenario, the effects of fluorescent and phosphorescent materials which absorb light and emits it for a longer time can be used. Also, this idea can extend the time in which the panel can produce electricity. Also, the initial studies show that certain materials which produce useful wavelength can be used. It should be noted that this is the future plan of this study and the expected energy production amount is not comparable to the direct sunlight. 

As a result, a tests on Low-E films with less than 50% visible light transmission and utilization of parabolic concentrators with concentration factor of 2, shown full compensation of the reduced power; as a future plan, synthesizing and using IR-Reflective coatings with free-electrons like ZnS coatings, as well as using films with higher visible light transmissions is going to be studied. Hereunder, the results for a low-emission film with 47% visible light transmission without a concentrator are drawn.
